# The Speech

*3 minutes before the board, round two*

---

## [0:00 - 0:45] 

Hi again - Rasmus Praestholm here from Mount Pleasant Ave, kids in Mount Pleasant and BMELC with paras. A-k-a leafblower beardo, maybe, who proposed a community exoskeleton last time.

I dropped off literal printed stacks of paper, filled in the feedback form, tried various ways to share links to the school proposal site I built in fragments of time as a busy nerd dad. As expected - nothing. And I'm not mad. I understand. Who has spare time these days?

The board are volunteers, now inundated with all this community engagement, while all the planning and decisions were probably done and final weeks and months ago. The school district is already struggling with cuts last year - how would they have time for anything? 

## [0:45 - 1:45] 

I didn't expect any replies, again like last year, because the system isn't set up for it. If elected and appointed officials don't have the time to do this work — and electing different ones doesn't change the time problem — then we have a systemic issue. These midnight meetings are part of the problem.

I made a condensed action plan after the last meeting as well, thinking a one-pager might make a difference. But then I stopped, when I figured it wouldn't. And I was considering despair, to be honest. I really thought about giving up.

But here's the thing: Despair is a luxury. The kids need us right now, and we're failing them. Odds are by the end of this evening, the cuts will be final, despite all the community energy in the room behind me.

## [1:45 - 2:30] 

So tonight I'm doing something I honestly don't feel ready for: launching the mildly cheekily named frontstate dot org. You hear plenty these days about hidden powers and behind-the-scenes forces working against ordinary people, but I believe in government's ability to do good. So instead of a hidden state — a FRONT state. Visible. Out in the open. In front of the powers that be.

The premise: If elected and appointed officials don't have the time to do this work as well as we'd like — and they don't — then ordinary communities have to build the coordination layer ourselves, adding the visibility and supporting process so we can make things better. The Exoskeleton.

The first working case study is right here. The budget crisis, now and later. Nothing in the published board "plan" explains why we won't be back here in a year after another 20% insurance increase, needing more cuts. That "plan" reads like a budget. Which it is. It isn't a plan.

## [2:30 - 3:00] 

The schools.frontstate.org site that I partially printed for last time is a preview. If we can figure this out for the school district, we can do it for healthcare costs more widely, for state funding formulas, and so forth.

No amount of fancy tooling will let me do this alone, nor should one person do so much or be depended on, just like we shouldn't depend on you 5 people to fix everything. I hope to work with new friends in the West Orange parent group, and also do still hope to eventually hear from the board, get the union involved, and so on.

I believe that enough visible data and process, paired with the collaboration of our beautifully diverse community, can lead to real plans.

We are not there right now. Please, to everybody here tonight, hang in there and help with something. I'm happy to coordinate, and will be out in the lobby. Site is at schools dot frontstate dot org. Thank you.
