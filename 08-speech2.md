# The Speech

*3 minutes before the board, round two*

---

## [0:00 - 0:45] 

Hi again - Rasmus Praestholm here from Mount Pleasant Ave, kids in Mount Pleasant and BMELC with paras. Aka leafblower beardo, maybe, who proposed a community exoskeleton last time.

I dropped off literal printed stacks of paper, filled in the feedback form, tried various ways to share links to the school proposal site I built in fragments of time as a busy nerd dad. As expected - nothing. And I'm not mad. I understand. Who has spare time these days?

The board are volunteers, now inundated with all this community engagement, while all the planning and decisions were probably done and final weeks and months ago. The school district is already struggling with cuts last year - how would they have time for anything? 

## [0:45 - 1:45] 

I didn't expect any replies, again like last year, because the system isn't set up for it. If elected and appointed officials don't have the time to do this work — and electing different ones doesn't change the time problem — then we have a systemic issue. Stay up like this past midnight is not helping.

I made a condensed action plan after the last meeting as well, thinking a one-pager might make a difference. But then I stopped, when I figured it wouldn't. And I was considering despair, to be honest. I really thought about giving up.

But here's the thing: Despair is a luxury. The kids need us right now, and we're failing them. Odds are that at the end of this evening that'll be final, despite all the community energy and potential in the room behind me.

## [1:45 - 2:30] 

So tonight I'm doing something I honestly don't feel ready for: launching a mildly cheekily named frontstate.org - because I do believe in government, and refuse to blame hidden evils behind the scene for problems that we should be able to solve outselves. 

The premise is "simple": If elected and appointed officials don't have the time to do this work well enough — and they don't — then ordinary communities have to build the coordination layer ourselves, in front of the existing state. Not replacing it. Just providing the visibility and coordination layer to make things better. The Exoskeleton.

The first working case study is right here. The budget crisis, now and in the future, since nothing in the big published board "plan" really explains why we won't be right back hear in a year after another 20% insurance increase, necesitating more cuts. That "plan" reads like a budget. Which it is. It isn't a plan.

## [2:30 - 3:00] 

The schools.frontstate.org site that I partially printed for last time is the preview. If we can figure this out for the school district, we can do it for healthcare costs, for state funding formulas, and so forth.

No amount of fancy tooling will let me do this alone, nor should one person do so much or be depended on, just like we shouldn't depend on you 5 people to fix everything. I hope to work with new friends in the West Orange parent group, but also do still hope to eventually hear from the board, get the union involved, and so on.

Ultimately I believe that if you get enough visible data and process ideas out in the open, and collaborate with a wide enough range of our beautifully diverse community, then the best decisions will become apparent.

We are not there right now. Please, to everybody here tonight, help with something. I'm happy to coordinate, and will be out in the lobby. Thank you.
